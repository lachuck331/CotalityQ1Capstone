Introduction

Wildfire prediction in California is a rare-event problem. This project builds a statewide, monthly 800m burn-probability framework using public climate, vegetation, terrain, and fire-perimeter data to support faster risk analysis than simulation-heavy pipelines.

🗺️

Statewide Scope

California domain, monthly resolution, 2000-2024 coverage on a harmonized 800m grid.

🧠

Modeling Goal

Estimate burn likelihood and rank higher-risk locations, not just optimize raw accuracy.

⚙️

Use Case

A reproducible workflow for interpretable wildfire risk signals in planning and resilience contexts.
